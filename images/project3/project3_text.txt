WORK 3 CHANDELIERS P &amp; R Chandelier Kiln fused glass, kiln cast glass, found metal and rebar ties, lighting 22 x 3 x 3’ 2021 Private Residence - Juneau, AK Created as a commissioned piece, this chandelier spans three floors, supported by an anchor chain, with each level containing a lighting element. The top floor viewed from the client's artist studio is created from multiple cast glass paint brushes suspended from a reclaimed shrimp pot. Attached to the shrimp pot and suspending the work at the levels below are rebar ties and kiln fused glass pendants designed in a netting pattern. The center lighting element, viewed from the kitchen and living areas is constructed from found metal and rebar ties welded in a circle creating a lightshade. The netting of rebar ties and glass pendants continues to the lower level and is built from a found metal basket and ulu shaped pendants. The entire structure weighs under 30 pounds, the heaviest element being the supporting anchor chain.  ALASKA ROBOTICS CHANDELIER AK Robotics Chand 2023 Kiln fused glass, non-traditional pate de verre, found metal, lighting 40 x 36 x 36” Created as a commissioned piece for Alaska Robotics Gallery, this chandelier is created of glass pendants using multiple kilned glass techniques and reclaimed found objects, inspired by the spirit of Alaska Robotics as a creative space. Utilizing waste sheet glass the comic book inspired glass panels are embellished with drawings by Pat Race (Gallery co-owner) and kiln fused with copper inclusions. The center is made of glass pencils constructed using a non-traditional pate-de-verre technique. The glass works are then suspended from a reused shrimp pot acting as a frame.
